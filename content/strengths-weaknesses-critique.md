## Weaknesses (draft — to be expanded and written to style guide)

1. **0g EU compliance margin — existential financial risk**
   Any EV sales shortfall in H2 2025 triggers ~€95 fine per gram per vehicle across 8M+ vehicles annually. No buffer. This is the single most urgent operational risk.

2. **Scope 3 SBTi targets not yet validated**
   BMW has full SBTi coverage across all scopes. VW's Scope 3 targets remain unvalidated — the #1 credibility gap versus competitors.

3. **CDP rating A− vs BMW's A**
   A marginal but meaningful gap in the transparency index. Achievable to close with enhanced Scope 3 disclosure.

4. **Net Zero 2050 — later than Mercedes-Benz 2039**
   Mercedes-Benz's Ambition 2039 is more aggressive. Although Mercedes backed off its electrification pledge, its net zero date is a stronger headline commitment.

5. **Lobbying inconsistencies**
   PwC and KPMG noted some inconsistencies between VW's stated climate positions and certain lobbying activities. Undermines trust rebuilt post-Dieselgate.

6. **Scope 3 Cat.11 — 94% of total emissions — no quick fix**
   Category 11 (Use of Sold Products) is 688 Mt CO₂e. The EV transition is the only meaningful lever, and it depends on consumer adoption rates outside VW's direct control.
